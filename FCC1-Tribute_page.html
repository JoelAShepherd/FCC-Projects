N T Wright

The Right Revd NT Wright speaking at the James Gregory Lecture, "Can a scientist believe in the resurrection?", held at St Andrews University on Thursday 20 December 2007.

Early life and career1:

  • Born in Morpeth, Northumberland on December 1st 1948
  • Attended Sedbergh School in Cumbria where he specialised in classics.
  • Studied Literae Humaniores at Oxford University, Exeter College , from 1968 to 1971.
  • From 1971-1975 he studied Anglican Ministry at Wycliffe Hall, Oxford, reciveing his (Oxford) MA.
  • Appointed to position of Junior Research Fellow at Merton College, Oxford in 1975.
  • Between 1978 and 1981 he was chaplain and fellow at Downing College, Cambridge.
  • In 1981 he recieved his DPhil from Merton College, his thesis topic being "The Messiah and the People of God: A Study in Pauline Theology with Particular Reference to the Argument of the Epistle to the Romans".
  • From 1981 he moved to Canada to become the assistant professor of New Testament studies at McGill University, Montreal, where he stayed until 1986.
  • Returning to England, he became chaplain, fellow and tutor at Worcester College and lecturer in New Testament in the University of Oxford (1986â€“93).
  • In 1994 he became Dean of Lichfield Cathedral.
  • In 2000 he became Canon Theologian at Westminster Abbey.
  • In 2003 he became Bishop of Durham.
  • In order to focus more fully on his academic and broadcasting work he became Research Professor of New Testament and Early Christianity at St Mary's College, St Andrews, in Scotland.
  • He is currently a senior research fellow at Wycliffe Hall, University of Oxford, starting there on 1st October 2019.

Books

NT Wright is a prolific writer with the (somewhat unusual) ability to write material for a broad range of audiances. Books such as those from the "... for everyone" series (and other published under the name Tom wright) are written to be easily understood and to unpack the bible in a way that makes sense to people without any scholarly or theological training. Books published under the name NT Wright dig deeper into theological issues and explore them more thoroughly, ranging from the popular "Surprised by Hope" to the magisterial "Christian Origins and the Question of God" series.
